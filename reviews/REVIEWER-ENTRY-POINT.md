# Reviewer Entry Point

If you are reviewing TRT, start here. The purpose of this page is to **narrow the critique to the one question that decides the programme's physics-facing branch** — not to ask you to evaluate all of TRT at once.

## What TRT is (and is not)

1. **TRT is not yet a physical theory.** It is a foundational-ontology research programme. Current status: a coherent position paper (v0.9), no corroborated novel fact yet — see [`3-prediction/appraisal.md`](../3-prediction/appraisal.md).
2. **The core thesis** is the identity **χ ≡ A(I∞ | L₃)**: actual reality is infinite informational potential constrained by finite logic through actualizing action.
3. **The conceptual center** is the four-tier engine — *representable ⊃ admissible ⊃ actual*, with *outcome-actual* the measured subset ([`1-hypothesis/hard-core.md`](../1-hypothesis/hard-core.md)).

## The one question

4. **The gating technical task is the formalization of co-admissibility** ([`2-theory/00-foundational/co-admissibility-formalization.md`](../2-theory/00-foundational/co-admissibility-formalization.md)):

   > Can co-admissibility be formalized as a **definite, computable, measure-independent** criterion `C(constraints) → admissible set`, with no free entanglement measure?

5. **The first proposed severe test** is the incompatible-decoherence-channel regime, where standard einselection is measure-dependent and TRT's criterion is meant not to be ([`3-prediction/co-admissibility-conjecture.md`](../3-prediction/co-admissibility-conjecture.md)). The near-term credibility path runs through this test, not the gravity branch.

## How to falsify the physics-facing branch

You do **not** need to refute the metaphysics. The physics branch falls if you can show that co-admissibility:

- cannot be made measure-independent (any formalization smuggles in a free measure), **or**
- collapses into the standard einselection optimization (predicts nothing new), **or**
- contradicts the experimentally accessible pointer structure in the cavity-field regime (including the analytically known critical coupling at which Fock-state optimality ends).

Any one of these, demonstrated, closes the branch — and is recorded as a **successful** contribution (see [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](../CONTRIBUTING.md)). A recorded refutation is progress, not failure.
