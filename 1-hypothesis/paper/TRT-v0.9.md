## Abstract

Triadic Reality Theory proposes that actualized physical reality is constituted by three co-requiring primitives: finite logical constraint, infinite informational potential, and actualizing action. These are denoted respectively as *L₃*, *I∞*, and *A*. The finite logical constraints *L₃* consist of identity, non-contradiction, and excluded middle. Infinite informational potential *I∞* denotes the unbounded domain of possible distinctions, relations, states, and configurations. Actualizing action *A* is the dynamic principle by which informational possibility becomes determinate actuality under logical constraint.

The central ontological identity of the theory is:

$$\chi \equiv \mathsf{A}(I_\infty \mid L_3)$$

where *χ* denotes actual reality. The corresponding variational stability condition is:

$$\delta \mathcal{S}[\chi] = 0$$

where *S* is the action functional over actualized, logically admissible information. On this view, physical reality is constrained information in action. Time emerges as the ordered relation among actualized states. Energy emerges as the measurable capacity for actualization and transformation within constrained information. Spacetime emerges as the stable relational geometry of actualized informational order. Observable phenomena are higher-order structures within *χ*.

TRT seeks to unify insights from logical realism, information theory, quantum information, constructor theory, variational physics, digital physics, and process metaphysics while maintaining ontological economy. It differs from computational reductionism by treating logic as prior to computation, information as richer than binary encoding, and action as the necessary bridge between possibility and actuality. TRT also predicts the effectiveness of mathematics in describing the physical world, treating that effectiveness as a consequence of the necessary logical and informational structure of actualized reality rather than as an unexplained anomaly. TRT is offered as a candidate foundational ontology and research program for physics, metaphysics, and synthetic intelligence, not as a completed physical theory.

## Terminological Note

Four terms carry technical weight throughout the paper and are fixed here to prevent misreading, particularly in §6.5.

*Representable.* Any well-formed structure in *I∞*, including structures that encode contradiction. Representability requires only well-formedness, not logical admissibility.

*Admissible.* A representable structure that survives the constraint of *L₃*. The admissible is the representable minus the contradiction-encoding structures. Mathematical structure and unmeasured quantum states are admissible.

*Actual.* A structure that actualizing action has realized, a member of *χ*. The actual is always determinate and always satisfies the law of excluded middle.

*Outcome-actual.* Reserved for the determinate result of a measurement. A quantum state prior to measurement is admissible and real but not outcome-actual: it is ontic structure at the level of constrained potentiality, not a realized outcome. The distinction between *real* and *outcome-actual* is what allows TRT to affirm the reality of the quantum state while denying that the unmeasured state is a determinate outcome.

## 1. Introduction

The question of why physical reality exists with intelligible order remains one of the central problems at the boundary of physics, metaphysics, and logic. Modern physics gives extraordinary mathematical descriptions of the behavior of physical systems, but those descriptions presuppose several deeper realities. They presuppose logical intelligibility. They presuppose distinguishable states. They presuppose lawful transformation. They presuppose that possibility and actuality are not identical.

Triadic Reality Theory, hereafter TRT, begins from those presuppositions and asks what must be minimally present for any physical reality to obtain.

The proposal is that physical reality cannot be grounded in law alone, information alone, or action alone. Law alone has no content to constrain. Information alone has no determinate admissibility. Action alone has no intelligible object or direction. Actualized reality requires the joint presence of finite logical constraint, infinite informational potential, and actualizing action, no one of which constitutes the actual in isolation.

TRT therefore begins with three co-requiring primitives: *L₃*, the finite logical laws of identity, non-contradiction, and excluded middle; *I∞*, infinite informational potential; and *A*, actualizing action.

From these, actual reality is defined as:

$$\chi \equiv \mathsf{A}(I_\infty \mid L_3)$$

This expression states that reality, denoted by the Greek letter chi, is the actualized domain of infinite informational potential under finite logical constraint through the primitive of action.

The theory can be compressed into a single sentence:

*Physical reality is constrained information in action.*

This position paper develops the primitives, the central formalism, the first-order derivational pathway, the relation between TRT and existing research programs, and the principal challenges facing the theory.

## 2. Motivating Problem

Physics ordinarily begins with already-formed structures: fields, particles, spacetime, laws, symmetries, states, measurements, and observers. These are then modeled with differential equations, Hilbert spaces, manifolds, Lagrangians, operators, or computational systems. This is operationally powerful. It leaves a foundational question open.

Why is there a lawful domain of physically actual states at all?

A theory of physical foundations must account for at least four features.

First, physical reality is logically intelligible. A state is itself and not another state in the same respect. A physically actual proposition cannot be both true and false in the same respect. A determinate state either obtains or does not obtain.

Second, physical reality is informationally structured. Physical systems are distinguishable. They bear states. They instantiate relations. They can be measured, encoded, transformed, and compared.

Third, physical reality is dynamic. Reality is not exhausted by a static set of possible states. Something accounts for actualization, transition, persistence, transformation, and ordering.

Fourth, physical reality exhibits stable extremal structure. Classical physics is deeply tied to variational principles. Quantum theory generalizes action-based reasoning through path-integral and amplitude formulations. General relativity and field theory both make action central to the structure of physical law.

TRT argues that these four features point to a triadic foundation. Logic gives admissibility. Information gives content. Action gives actualization. Reality is their irreducible synthesis.

## 3. The Three Co-Primitives

### 3.1 L₃: Finite Logical Constraint

The first primitive is *L₃*, the finite set of logical laws without which determinate reality cannot be coherently specified.

$$L_3 = \{\text{Identity},\ \text{Non-Contradiction},\ \text{Excluded Middle}\}$$

The law of identity states that a thing is itself: *P = P*. The law of non-contradiction states that a proposition and its negation cannot both be true in the same respect: *¬(P ∧ ¬P)*. The law of excluded middle states that a determinate proposition either obtains or does not obtain: *P ∨ ¬P*.

In TRT, these laws are not merely human conventions, psychological habits, linguistic rules, or descriptions of neural behavior. They are admissibility constraints. They mark the boundary between coherent actuality and incoherent pseudo-state.

This does not mean every quantum description picks out a determinate actual state. A system may be unresolved relative to actualization. A quantum state may encode the structured potential for several outcomes relative to a measurement context. Yet an unresolved description is not an ontological contradiction. The distinction TRT requires is not between the known and the unknown but between the actual and the merely admissible. A system whose actualization is unresolved is not thereby a determinate actual state that is both *P* and not-*P* in the same respect. It is not yet a determinate actual state at all. The full development of this point, including its relation to the Pusey-Barrett-Rudolph theorem, is given in §6.5.

Thus *L₃* functions as the formal admissibility condition for physical actuality. A candidate physical state *s* is admissible only if *s ∈ A_{L₃}*, where *A_{L₃}* denotes the set of logically admissible states.

### 3.2 I∞: Infinite Informational Potential

The second primitive is *I∞*, infinite informational potential.

Information in TRT is not reduced to Shannon information, binary encoding, digital bits, computation, or semantic content. Those are downstream forms of information. TRT uses information in a more primitive ontological sense: the possibility of distinction, relation, configuration, state, and structure.

A domain with no possible distinctions is indistinguishable from nothing with respect to physical intelligibility. To be physically real, a state must be differentiable from other states. It must have determinate relations. It must stand within an order of possible comparison.

Thus *I∞* denotes the unbounded domain of possible distinctions and relations prior to finite actualization.

$$I_\infty = \{\text{all possible distinctions, relations, states, and configurations}\}$$

The subscript ∞ does not imply that an actually instantiated physical universe contains an infinite quantity of realized information. It means that informational potential is not intrinsically exhausted by any finite actual state. Finite physical systems instantiate finite informational structures. The domain of possible informational configurations remains unbounded with respect to actualization.

TRT therefore distinguishes *I∞* (infinite informational potential), *I_adm* (logically admissible information), and *I_actual* (actualized information within *χ*). The logical constraint relation is:

$$I_{\text{adm}} = I_\infty \mid L_3$$

That is, admissible information is infinite informational potential constrained by the finite laws of logic.

### 3.3 A: Actualizing Action

The third primitive is *A*, actualizing action.

Action in TRT is more primitive than motion, force, temporal change, or physical work. Motion presupposes spacetime. Force presupposes dynamical law. Work presupposes energy transfer across an already actual physical system. TRT places *A* beneath these derivative categories.

*A* is the principle by which logically admissible informational possibility becomes determinate actuality:

$$\mathsf{A}: I_{\text{adm}} \rightarrow I_{\text{actual}}$$

The result is actual reality:

$$\chi \equiv \mathsf{A}(I_\infty \mid L_3)$$

Action, in this ontological sense, is the bridge between possibility and actuality. Without action, informational potential remains merely possible. Without information, action has no object. Without logic, action has no coherent admissibility conditions.

The use of action also aligns TRT with the deep structure of physics. Classical mechanics, field theory, quantum mechanics, and general relativity all grant action a central role. TRT does not simply borrow the physics concept and project it backward. Rather, it proposes that the prominence of action in physical theory reflects a deeper ontological role.

To avoid ambiguity, TRT distinguishes between *A*, the ontological primitive of actualizing action, and *S*, the action functional used in physical modeling. The primitive *A* actualizes reality. The functional *S* describes the stability and extremal structure of actualized reality.

### 3.4 The Representable, the Admissible, and the Actual

The relation between *I∞* and *L₃* introduced above carries a structural consequence that deserves explicit statement, because it determines how TRT locates logical paradox and how it answers the claim that some contradictions are true. TRT distinguishes three tiers within informational structure.

The first tier is the *representable*. *I∞*, infinite informational potential, contains every well-formed distinction, relation, and configuration. This includes structures that encode contradiction: the Liar sentence, Russell's set, any formula of the form *P ∧ ¬P*. Such structures are available *as objects of representation*. The string that says of itself that it is false is a well-formed, individuable structure. The unrestricted comprehension that yields Russell's set is a statable principle. A contradiction can be written, named, and reasoned about. At the level of representation, it is a perfectly good object.

The second tier is the *admissible*. The logically admissible information is the subdomain of *I∞* that survives the constraint of *L₃*:

$$I_{\text{adm}} = I_\infty \mid L_3$$

Here *L₃* operates as a filter. The contradiction-encoding structures of the first tier are representable but not admissible. They are present in *I∞* and excluded from *I_adm*. The constraint does not erase them from representation. It withholds them from actualization.

The third tier is the *actual*. Actualizing action realizes the actual from the admissible:

$$\chi = \mathsf{A}(I_{\text{adm}})$$

Only structures that have passed the filter are available for actualization. A contradiction-encoding structure, excluded at the second tier, never reaches the third.

The architecture therefore places logical paradox precisely. A contradiction is real at the level of representation and excluded at the gate between the representable and the actual. It never actualizes, not because it cannot be written, but because *L₃* governs what *A* can actualize. The difference between a contradiction and the Mandelbrot set, on this account, is smaller than it first appears. Both are tier-one or tier-two structures that do not, of themselves, actualize. The Mandelbrot set is admissible and unactualized; a true contradiction is representable, inadmissible, and therefore actualizable under no condition whatever.

#### 3.4.1 The Location of Dialetheism

This locates the dialetheist's claim rather than denying it. Dialetheism holds that some contradictions are true, on the strength of semantic and set-theoretic paradoxes: the Liar, Russell's set, the boundaries of vague predicates. TRT grants that these are genuine contradictory structures. It observes where they live. Every such case is a structure of the first tier, the representable, where self-reference and unrestricted comprehension generate contradiction. None has ever been exhibited as a structure of the third tier, an actualized physical state instantiating *P ∧ ¬P* in the same respect.

The concession costs TRT nothing, because TRT is an ontology of the actual. Dialetheism may stand as a logic of the representable, an account of how representation behaves when self-reference and unrestricted comprehension are permitted. It has no purchase on *χ*. The standing challenge TRT issues is correspondingly precise: exhibit one actualized physical state, one determinate measurement outcome, that instantiates a contradiction in the same respect. Not a sentence about a state, not an idealized limit, not a self-referential representation. An actual state. The empirical record of physics contains none. Apparent quantum cases are unresolved superposition, addressed in §3.1 and §6.5, which is incompleteness of actualized description relative to a measurement context, not a determinate state that is both *P* and *¬P*. Until such a state is produced, dialetheism bears on the logic of representation and not on the ontology of the actual.

#### 3.4.2 A Consequence: The Discipline of Foundations

The three-tier structure yields a consequence concerning the history of mathematics itself, offered with lower confidence than the dialetheism result above and flagged as such.

If consistent mathematics is *I∞* filtered by *L₃*, then naive representation, which permits unrestricted self-reference and comprehension, will generate structures that the filter must exclude. One would therefore expect that any sufficiently ambitious attempt to found mathematics on unrestricted representation would encounter contradiction, and that the repair would take the form of restricting representation to recover admissibility.

This is what the foundational crisis of the early twentieth century records. Naive set theory permitted Russell's construction. The repair, in Russell's type theory and in the axiomatic restriction of comprehension within Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory, was precisely the disciplined exclusion of representable structures that *L₃* does not admit. On the TRT reading, the history of foundations is the discovery of the gate: the determination, case by case, of which representable structures are logically admissible. That consistent mathematics had to be won by restricting naive representation, rather than arriving intact, is what the three-tier structure leads one to expect.

This claim is weaker than the effectiveness result of §9.5. The history of foundations admits competing readings, and intuitionist and formalist accounts explain the restriction of comprehension on other grounds. TRT does not claim its reading is the only available one. It claims that the three-tier structure renders the restriction of naive representation unsurprising, where on a view that did not distinguish the representable from the admissible it would stand as a bare historical accident. The confidence here is moderate, not high. The result is offered as coherent and suggestive, not as established.

## 5. The Fundamental Formalism

The central identity of TRT is:

$$\chi \equiv \mathsf{A}(I_\infty \mid L_3)$$

The corresponding variational principle is:

$$\delta \mathcal{S}[\chi] = 0$$

The first equation is ontological. The second is physical. The ontological equation defines what actual reality is. The variational equation describes how physically realized reality stabilizes.

The sequence is:

$$I_\infty \rightarrow I_{\text{adm}} \rightarrow I_{\text{actual}} \rightarrow \chi$$

where *I_adm = I∞ | L₃* and *I_actual = A(I_adm)*, therefore *χ = I_actual* understood as the complete actualized domain.

The action functional is then defined over *χ*:

$$\mathcal{S}[\chi] = \int_\Omega \mathcal{L}(\chi, \partial\chi, L_3)\, d\Omega$$

where *L* is a Lagrangian density over actualized reality, *∂χ* denotes relational variation within actualized structure, and *dΩ* denotes the appropriate measure over the emergent domain. The stationarity condition is *δS[χ] = 0*.

This gives a foundational interpretation of variational physics. Physical laws arise as stable extremal structures of constrained information in action.

> **Caveat (added in revision).** The variational apparatus of this section is, at present, a desideratum rather than a derivation. The Lagrangian density *L* has no specified form, *∂χ* and the measure *dΩ* are undefined, and no equations of motion are generated. TRT conjectures that actualized reality exhibits extremal structure of this kind; the recovery of a definite *L* and the derivation of physical equations from it are open problems, recorded in §12. The expressions above mark the target of the program, not a result it has reached.

## 6. Derivational Pathway

TRT is a foundational ontology and research program. It does not yet claim a complete derivation of all physics. It does claim that major physical categories can be placed on a coherent derivational pathway from the triadic foundation.

### 6.1 Time

In TRT, time is not primitive. Time emerges as relational ordering among actualized states. Let *χ_i* and *χ_j* denote distinguishable actualized reality-states. If *A* orders these states through actualization, then time is the relational ordering structure generated by action over constrained information:

$$t \sim \mathrm{Ord}_{\mathsf{A}}(\chi_i, \chi_j)$$

Time is therefore neither an independent container nor a mere illusion. It is the ordered relation of actualization. A reality-state does not merely sit in time; temporal ordering is generated by the ordered actualization of reality-states.

### 6.2 Energy

Energy is commonly understood as the capacity to do work. That definition presupposes an already actual physical system. TRT seeks a more primitive description: energy is the measurable capacity for actualization, persistence, and transformation within constrained information.

$$E \sim \frac{\Delta \mathcal{S}}{\Delta t}$$

This aligns with the dimensional relationship between action, energy, and time: *[S] = [E][t]*. Energy is therefore action-rate within an emergent temporal order. At the ontological level, energy is actualized action expressed as measurable transformation capacity.

### 6.3 Spacetime

Spacetime is not primitive in TRT. It emerges as the stable relational geometry of actualized information. If actualized states bear relations of adjacency, ordering, transformation, and constraint, then spacetime is the geometry of those relations:

$$g_{\mu\nu} \sim \mathrm{Geom}(\mathrm{Rel}(\chi))$$

where *g_{μν}* denotes the emergent metric structure and *Rel(χ)* denotes the relational structure of actualized reality. This provides a conceptual bridge to quantum gravity research programs that treat spacetime as emergent from entanglement, information, causal order, or relational structure. TRT adds that such emergence must be logically admissible and action-governed.

### 6.4 Physical Law

Physical law emerges as stable regularity within the variational structure of *χ*. A physical law is not merely a human description of pattern; it is a stable constraint relation within actualized reality:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{phys}} \subset \mathrm{Stab}(\chi)$$

where *Stab(χ)* denotes stable structures of actualized reality. Physical law is therefore downstream from the triad. Logic establishes admissibility. Information supplies possible structure. Action actualizes stable order.


### 6.5 Quantum States and the Boundary of Actualization

The quantum state has a precise location within the three-tier structure of §3.4. It is real, but not outcome-actual. It is a structure of the admissible, *I_adm*, a real physical feature at the level of constrained potentiality, standing at the boundary where actualizing action resolves admissible potential into a determinate outcome. Superposition names that boundary condition: admissible physical structure prior to the crossing into outcome-actuality.

This placement resolves the apparent conflict between quantum indeterminacy and the law of excluded middle, and it does so without weakening the law.

#### 6.5.1 The State as Admissible Structure

A quantum state is a vector in a Hilbert space:

$$|\psi\rangle \in \mathcal{H}_{\text{adm}}$$

Considered as mathematical structure, *|ψ⟩* is fully determinate. It is a definite vector. Its unitary evolution under the Schrödinger equation is deterministic. Nothing about *|ψ⟩* as structure is fuzzy, unknown, or indeterminate. It is an element of the admissible tier, *I_adm*, and it behaves there as admissible structure behaves: consistently, determinately, and without being outcome-actual.

What is unresolved is not the structure but the actualization. The state encodes the structured potential for several actual outcomes. It does not specify which outcome actualizes. That non-specification is not a defect in the structure and not a gap in an observer's knowledge. It is the structure standing at the threshold of actuality, prior to the action that would resolve it.

The quantum state is therefore not merely epistemic, but neither is it outcome-actual. It is ontic admissible structure: a real physical feature at the level of constrained potentiality, not a hidden determinate value and not yet a realized outcome.

Superposition, then, is not a state that is both *P* and not-*P*. It is admissible structure that has not yet been actualized into either. The difference is the difference between the second and third tiers of §3.4. A superposed state belongs to the admissible. An outcome belongs to the actual.

#### 6.5.2 Measurement as the Boundary Crossing

Measurement is the actualization event. It is *A* operating across the boundary between the admissible and the actual, resolving the structured potential of *|ψ⟩* into a determinate outcome:

$$|\psi\rangle \rightarrow \chi_{\text{obs}}$$

The Born rule is the law of that crossing. It assigns to each admissible branch the probability with which actualizing action realizes it. The rule is the bridge between the mathematical layer, where *|ψ⟩* lives and evolves, and the actual layer, where determinate outcomes obtain. The Logical Resolution Model, LRM, developed in §6.6 and named in §7, is the model of this crossing.

The measured system is determinately *P* or determinately not-*P*. No proposition over the actual outcome is both affirmed and denied. The law governs propositions over the actual, and the actual is exactly what measurement produces.

#### 6.5.3 Excluded Middle and the Quantum State

A natural objection holds that superposition violates the law of excluded middle, since a superposed system appears to be neither determinately *P* nor determinately not-*P*, and that this violation impugns *L₃*.

The objection mislocates the law. Excluded middle governs propositions over actualized states. It requires that for every actual state, the proposition affirming it determinately holds or determinately fails. A superposed *|ψ⟩* is not an actualized state. It is admissible structure at the boundary of actualization. The disjunction *P ∨ ¬P* applies to propositions over the actual outcome, and the actual outcome always satisfies it. Before the boundary crossing there is no actual state to which such propositions apply, and the superposed structure is no more a violation of excluded middle than an unactualized mathematical object is. A mathematical structure such as the Mandelbrot set may be determinate as structure without being actualized as a physical object. Likewise, on TRT, a quantum state may be determinate as admissible physical structure without being actualized as a determinate measurement outcome. In neither case is there an actual state of which contradictory propositions both hold.

This preserves *L₃* intact. The law is not scoped down, qualified, or suspended for quantum systems. It governs propositions over the actual, as it always has, and the quantum state is simply not a member of the actual until measurement makes it one.

#### 6.5.4 The Reality of the State and the Pusey-Barrett-Rudolph Theorem

The placement above must answer a strong result. Pusey, Barrett, and Rudolph (2012) proved that, under the assumption of preparation independence, the quantum state cannot be interpreted as mere knowledge about an underlying determinate physical state. If two distinct quantum states could correspond to the same underlying reality with overlapping probability, a contradiction with quantum predictions follows. The theorem rules out the psi-epistemic program: the view that *|ψ⟩* represents an observer's ignorance of a definite value the system already possesses.

TRT's boundary reading is not psi-epistemic, and the theorem does not touch it. TRT does not hold that *|ψ⟩* is ignorance of a determinate actual value. It holds that *|ψ⟩* is real admissible structure standing at the boundary of actualization. There is no determinate actual value beneath the superposition of which the observer is ignorant. There is admissible structure that has not yet been actualized. This is a psi-ontic position: the state is a real physical feature of the system, not a state of knowledge about a deeper reality. The theorem is consistent with psi-ontic readings, and it is best understood as an argument for exactly the kind of view TRT takes. The reality TRT assigns to *|ψ⟩* is the reality of the admissible tier, *I_adm*, which is genuine structure and not nothing, and the fact that a given system is governed by *this* *|ψ⟩* rather than another is a real fact about the system.

The theorem therefore strengthens rather than threatens the present account. It closes the epistemic reading that an earlier formulation of §3.1 mistakenly adopted, and it leaves standing the boundary reading developed here.

#### 6.5.5 What Remains Open

This account locates the measurement problem precisely. It does not dissolve it.

TRT specifies where measurement sits in its ontology: the crossing of the boundary between admissible structure and actualized outcome, performed by *A*, governed at the level of probability by the Born rule. It does not thereby explain why the Born rule takes the form it does, why actualizing action resolves one admissible branch rather than another on a given occasion, or what, if anything, determines the particular outcome beyond its probability. These are the hard residues of the measurement problem, and TRT inherits them. What it offers is a precise relocation: the problem is the problem of the boundary crossing, not a problem about hidden values beneath the state, nor a problem about observer knowledge. The Born rule is named as the law of the crossing, and the derivation of that law from the triadic structure, rather than its assumption, is left as an open problem and recorded in §12.

The honest claim is that TRT clarifies the location and character of the measurement problem and recasts it as a question about the actualization boundary. The honest disclaimer is that clarifying a problem's location is not solving it.

### 6.6 The Logical Resolution Model

The Logical Resolution Model specifies the crossing named in §6.5. The proposal has two parts, and they carry very different evidential weight. The crossing decomposes into two stages: the first runs from the representable to the admissible, *I∞ → I_adm*; the second runs from the admissible to the actual, *I_adm → χ*. LRM makes a substantive claim about the first stage and an honest concession about the second.

#### 6.6.1 The First Stage: Resolution to the Admissible Set

When a quantum system couples to physical state variables, an apparatus, an environment, a set of conserved quantities, the interaction does not leave every mathematically representable structure standing. The coupling imposes constraints, and most of the infinite representable structure cannot satisfy them. What survives is the set of structures that remain logically co-admissible under the full constraint imposed by the physical variables in interaction.

This survival criterion is the content of the claim that measurement is that which survives logical interaction with the state variables. The survivors are not selected by an observer. They are selected by the logical compatibility of candidate structures with the totality of constraints the actual physical variables impose. The criterion is co-admissibility rather than mere consistency: the survivors form the maximal set of structures that can *jointly* satisfy *L₃* given the variables, not merely the structures individually consistent with any one constraint.

This first-stage claim recovers, in TRT's terms, a result that decoherence theory establishes independently. Environmental monitoring suppresses coherence between a preferred set of states and bans the rest of Hilbert space from stable classical existence. The pointer states that survive einselection are the physically robust survivors of interaction with the environment. TRT reads these as the co-admissible set: the structures that survive logical interaction with the physical variables. The agreement with decoherence is the reason the first-stage claim is offered with moderate confidence. It is not a new prediction. It is a re-description, in logical-ontological terms, of selection that decoherence already explains dynamically.

The value of the re-description, if it has value, is twofold. It grounds the selection in *L₃* rather than in a choice of entanglement measure, and it removes the circularity that has been charged against the einselection program, since co-admissibility is defined by logical compatibility with the variables rather than by stability under a dynamics whose preferred basis the argument is trying to explain. Whether this grounding is more than re-description is exactly the question the conjecture of §13.1 is designed to decide.

#### 6.6.2 The Second Stage: Actualization of the Outcome

The first stage resolves the representable to the co-admissible set. In general this set has more than one member. The number-basis pointer states of a decohered field are a co-admissible set, not a single state. Something further selects the one outcome that actualizes on a given occasion.

LRM does not explain that final selection. The transition from the co-admissible set to the single actual outcome, *I_adm → χ*, is the residual actualization event, the operation of *A* on an occasion. TRT locates it, names it, and assigns it the Born rule as its probabilistic law, but it does not derive the Born measure and does not say what fixes the particular outcome beyond its probability. This is the hard residue of the measurement problem, recorded as open in §12. The factoring is the contribution: the first stage is logical and, TRT claims, explicable; the second stage is the genuine remainder, and TRT does not pretend to have removed it.

It is not to be confused with generic logical realism as a stance in the philosophy of logic; LRT is the named layer of the present program that formalizes one of TRT's three co-requiring constituents: the governance that finite logical constraint, *L₃*, exerts over what can be actualized.

### 7.1 The Status of Logical Constraint Within TRT

LRT's governing commitment, that physical reality is logically constrained, holds a particular status within TRT. It is not a derived theorem, and it is not an axiom in the ordinary sense of a starting assumption that could have been chosen otherwise. It is a precondition whose denial is incoherent.

An ordinary axiom can be dropped to yield a consistent alternative system. The parallel postulate is an axiom in this sense: its denial gives consistent non-Euclidean geometry. *L₃* is not an axiom of that kind. One cannot construct the non-*L₃* alternative the way one constructs non-Euclidean space, because the construction itself uses identity and non-contradiction. The denial of *L₃* is self-refuting. To assert that some actual thing is both *F* and not-*F* in the same respect, one must hold *F* to its meaning, which is identity, and must treat the assertion as true and not also false, which is non-contradiction. The denial deploys the laws it purports to deny.

This is the status Aristotle assigned to the principle of non-contradiction in *Metaphysics* Γ. The principle cannot be proved without presupposing it, yet its denial can be shown to collapse, a demonstration by refutation rather than by derivation. LRT's logical commitment inherits that status. It is foundational not because TRT posits it freely but because nothing actual could fail to conform to it, and no coherent denial of it can be stated.

The standing objection to this position, dialetheism, holds that some contradictions are true. TRT's answer is structural and is developed in §3.4: the contradictions dialetheism adduces are real structures of the representable tier and are excluded at the gate between the representable and the actual. They never actualize. The objection therefore bears on the logic of representation and not on the ontology of *χ*, and LRT's constraint stands at the level it claims to govern.

A precision is required here, because the claim is easily overstated. *L₃* governs the *form* of the actual. It does not entail the *fact* of the actual. A domain of pure unactualized possibility, the admissible without actualization, violates no logical law. Mathematical structure is not a contradiction. *L₃* therefore cannot be what forces actualization, and LRT does not claim that it does. Logical constraint settles what *A* may actualize, not whether *A* actualizes. The constraint and the actualization remain distinct, which is precisely why *L₃* and *A* are co-requiring constituents rather than one constituent wearing two names. This preserves the result of §9.4, that logic alone is sterile: a system governed only by *L₃*, with no *I∞* to supply content and no *A* to actualize, yields no determinate reality at all.

### 7.2 What the Relocation Settles

Positioning LRT as the formalization of the *L₃* constituent settles two questions that a reader carries from the earlier sections.

It settles what TRT adds beyond LRT. LRT formalizes one constituent. TRT adds *I∞* and *A* as co-requiring constituents alongside *L₃*, together with the actualization relation that yields *χ*. TRT is broader because logical constraint, however foundational, governs the admissible without supplying either its content or its actuality. The added constituents are not downstream of logic. They are co-required with it.

It settles the relation between the logical commitment and the physics. LRT is the program through which the inviolable logical constraint is given physical formalization, the layer at which *L₃*'s governance is developed into a model of admissible physical states. LRM then operates within that program to model measurement as resolution. The single chain, TRT to LRT to LRM to predictions, carries the development from ontology through constraint to measurement to test, with no redundant layer between them.

## 8. Relation to Existing Research Programs

TRT intersects with several existing traditions while remaining distinct.

### 8.1 Information Theory

Claude Shannon's mathematical theory of communication treated information in terms of uncertainty, entropy, signal transmission, and noise within a communication system. This was a technical and operational theory of communication, not a complete ontology of reality. TRT affirms the importance of information but moves beneath communication theory. Shannon information presupposes distinguishable states, possible alternatives, and structured encoding. TRT asks what makes such distinguishability possible at all. Shannon information is therefore a downstream formalization within actualized informational systems. TRT's *I∞* is more primitive.

### 8.2 Digital Physics

Digital physics and related "it from bit" proposals suggest that physical reality is fundamentally informational or computational. These views correctly perceive that matter is not metaphysically basic in the older materialist sense. TRT differs by refusing to reduce reality to bits or computation. Computation presupposes logical structure, distinguishable states, transition rules, and actual operations. These are already triadic. Computation therefore belongs within *χ*. It is not the final primitive. TRT can absorb the insight of digital physics while avoiding the reduction of ontology to binary encoding.

### 8.3 Quantum Information

Quantum information theory shows that information is not merely a convenient description laid on top of physics. Information is inseparable from the structure of quantum systems, measurement, entanglement, and physical possibility. TRT agrees. It then asks what grounds the admissibility and actualization of quantum informational structure. The answer is the triad: *L₃*, *I∞*, and *A*.

### 8.4 Constructor Theory

Constructor theory frames physics in terms of possible and impossible transformations. This is strongly congenial to TRT because it treats possibility, impossibility, and transformation as fundamental categories. TRT supplies an ontological foundation beneath that distinction. Possibility and impossibility require logical admissibility. Transformations require informational states and actualizing action. Thus constructor theory can be read as a physics-level expression of the deeper TRT structure.

### 8.5 Variational Physics

The action principle is one of the most powerful structures in physics. Classical mechanics, quantum mechanics, field theory, and relativity all make profound use of action. TRT interprets this as evidence that action is not an accidental mathematical convenience but a sign of a deeper ontological role. Physical systems exhibit stationary-action structure because actualized reality is constrained information in action. This does not eliminate standard variational physics; it grounds it.

> **Caveat (added in revision).** The inference from the prominence of the action functional *S* in physics to the ontological depth of the primitive *A* must be made with care, since §3.3 distinguishes the two. The prominence of *S* is suggestive of, but does not by itself establish, the ontological role claimed for *A*. The argument here is offered as motivation, not proof, and the bridge between *S* and *A* is among the open problems of §12.

### 8.6 Process Philosophy

Process metaphysics has long emphasized becoming, event, and activity rather than static substance. TRT shares the intuition that actuality is dynamic. However, TRT resists a pure process ontology because process alone does not supply logical admissibility or informational content. The triadic structure is essential. Action requires information. Information requires logical constraint. Logic requires informational content to constrain if reality is to be actual.

## 9. Ontological Economy

TRT proposes three co-primitives because fewer than three appear insufficient to constitute actualized reality. This section states the economy claim, defends it against the charge of circularity, specifies the kind of necessity TRT attributes to the triad, and draws out one standing consequence: the effectiveness of mathematics in describing the physical world.

Throughout, the object of analysis is *χ*, actualized reality. TRT does not here argue that an actual world exists necessarily. It analyzes what any actually real thing necessarily involves. The necessity at issue is the necessity of the constituents given actuality, not the necessity of actuality itself.

### 9.1 The Triad and Its Co-Requirement

The three co-primitives are mutually co-requiring. Logical constraint without informational content constrains nothing. Informational potential without logical admissibility supplies no criterion of coherent actuality. Actualizing action without logically admissible information has no determinate object to actualize. None of the three yields actualized reality in isolation. Their joint presence yields *χ*.

This co-requirement is a substantive claim about the structure of the actual. It is not a claim that the three cannot be characterized apart from one another. The distinction is essential to the next subsection. Each primitive can be characterized on its own terms. The laws of logic can be stated as pure form, without reference to information or action. Informational potential can be characterized as the bare possibility of distinction, without reference to logic or action. Actualizing action can be characterized as the conferral of actuality on the admissible, without first defining logic or information. That each can be characterized alone, while none obtains alone, is the signature of correlative constituents rather than circular definitions.

### 9.2 The Circularity Objection and Its Resolution

State the objection in its strongest form. If the three are genuinely primitive, each should be specifiable without the others. Yet TRT presents them together, and the central identity *χ ≡ A(I∞ | L₃)* exhibits all three in a single expression. The actualization relation references admissible information, which references logic and content together. A critic may conclude that the primitives appear in one another's specifications, that the dependency graph contains a cycle, and that a cyclic dependency among claimed primitives is a defect.

The objection conflates two relations that must be kept distinct.

The first is *definitional dependence*. Term *X* depends definitionally on *Y* when one cannot say what *X* is without a prior definition of *Y*. A set of terms each of which depends definitionally on the others is viciously circular, because no term can be specified first and the whole set fails to acquire content. This is a genuine defect.

The second is *metaphysical co-requirement*. *X* and *Y* co-require when neither obtains without the other, while each remains characterizable on its own terms. This relation is common in respectable metaphysics and carries no circularity. There is no convex surface without a concave one, yet convexity is characterizable without invoking concavity in its definition. Up does not obtain without down, determinables do not obtain without determinates, yet neither pair is defined circularly. Aristotle treated such correlatives, *ta pros ti*, as a standard category. Correlatives co-require in being while remaining independently specifiable in definition.

TRT's primitives stand in the second relation, not the first. The check is direct. Trace the definitional dependency graph: each primitive is characterized in its own terms, as set out in §9.1, so the graph carries no definitional edges among the three. Audit for definitional self-reference: none is present, since no primitive appears in its own definiens or in the definiens of another. The only dependency edge in the theory runs from the triad to its output, *χ*. That graph is acyclic.

What looked like a cycle was the co-requirement relation misread as a definitional one. The three are co-requiring constituents of actualized reality. Their mutual presupposition is a thesis about how the actual is built, not a circularity in how the theory is specified.

### 9.3 The Necessity of the Triad

TRT holds that the three are *necessary* constituents of actualized reality, and that this necessity is *synthetic* rather than analytic. The claim is not that "actualized reality" is defined as logically-constrained information in action, so that the triad follows by stipulation. The claim is that the triadic structure is a substantive feature of the actual, discovered rather than packed into a definition, such that nothing actual could lack any of the three.

A synthetic necessity claim owes an argument that the structure could not have been otherwise. The argument proceeds by attempting to describe an actually real thing lacking one constituent and showing that the description fails to pick out anything actual. The three attempts fail in three distinct ways, and the distinctness of the failures is itself the evidence that the necessity is synthetic. Were the triad merely analytic, removal of any constituent would produce the same failure, a violation of the definition, wearing three labels. It does not. Three independently recognizable lacks appear.

Remove logical constraint. Posit an actual thing that is both *F* and not-*F* in the same respect. The specification cancels itself. There is no determinate way the thing is, because the description supplies and withdraws the same content in one breath. What fails is *determinacy*.

Remove informational potential. Posit an actual thing bearing no distinctions and no relations, differentiable from nothing. There is no respect in which it differs from anything else or from its own absence. There is no fact about which thing it is. What fails is *differentiation*, the identity a thing has through its distinctions.

Remove actualizing action. Posit a fully specified, logically admissible informational structure that is not actual. The Mandelbrot set is such a structure. So is any unrealized possible world, and any object of pure mathematics. It is real as structure. It does nothing, occupies no causal or temporal order, and belongs to no actual history. What fails is *actuality* itself.

Three constituents, three distinct failure modes, three independently recognizable lacks. Determinacy, differentiation, and actuality can be subtracted one at a time, and each subtraction removes something specific from the actual. That they come apart is the synthetic signature.

This argument carries a load-bearing premise that TRT states explicitly rather than assuming in silence. *Actuality is a genuine status distinct from possibility.* The removal-of-action case depends on it. A modal realist who holds that the merely possible is as real as the actual will deny that the Mandelbrot set's lack of actuality is any lack at all, and will read A's necessity as true only by TRT's labeling of one region of being as "actual." TRT does not share that view. It treats the actual as a real status over and above admissible structure, the status that the present physical world has and that an unrealized possibility does not; §3.4 develops the three-tier structure on which this distinction rests. This is a metaphysical posit, not a result derived within the theory. It is the posit under which most working physicists operate, since physics concerns itself with the world that obtains rather than with the full space of worlds that might have. TRT adopts it openly. A reader who rejects it will read the necessity of A as conditional on a definition rather than synthetic; a reader who accepts it will read it as the synthetic structure of the actual. The physical content developed elsewhere in the theory is unchanged either way, which is the point of stating the posit rather than burying it.

The necessity, then, is conditional and synthetic. Given that something is actually real, it necessarily exhibits all three constituents. This stops short of claiming that actuality itself obtains necessarily, a stronger and more contested thesis that TRT does not need and does not assert here.

### 9.4 Why Not Fewer

The economy of the theory is the claim that the actual cannot be constituted by fewer than these three.

Logic alone is sterile. It supplies the form of admissibility but no content for admissibility to range over.

Information alone is indeterminate. It supplies possible distinction but no criterion separating coherent actuality from incoherent pseudo-state.

Action alone is blind. It supplies the conferral of actuality but no object to actualize and no condition on what is actualized.

The three are jointly required for the actual, and no two suffice. This is the precise sense in which the triad is minimal. The argument is not aesthetic preference for symmetry. It is the result of §9.3: each constituent answers to a distinct and independently recognizable requirement of actuality, and the removal of any one leaves no path from constituents to *χ*.

$$(L_3,\ I_\infty,\ \mathsf{A}) \Longrightarrow \chi$$

### 9.5 A Standing Consequence: The Effectiveness of Mathematics

The effectiveness of mathematics in describing physical reality is, on many ontologies, a standing puzzle. Wigner (1960) framed it as an unreasonable effectiveness, a fit between abstract mathematical structure and the physical world that those ontologies treat as brute and unexplained. Why should the structures generated by pure logical and informational reasoning map onto the behavior of actual physical systems at all?

TRT removes the puzzle by predicting the fit. If actualized reality is necessarily logically constrained and informationally structured, in the synthetic sense established in §9.3, then mathematics is not an external descriptive instrument that happens to apply. Mathematics is what logical constraint operating on informational potential generates. It is the structure of the admissible. Actualized reality, being necessarily constituted by logical constraint and informational potential, necessarily takes a form that mathematics describes, because mathematics is the articulation of exactly that constraint on exactly that potential.

The effectiveness of mathematics is therefore a consequence of the theory rather than an anomaly it must accommodate. Two of TRT's three necessary constituents are precisely the constituents from which mathematics arises. That actualized reality is mathematically describable is what TRT leads one to expect. The "unreasonable" effectiveness is, on this account, the reasonable and predicted effectiveness of a description that tracks the necessary logical-informational structure of the actual.

This is offered as a consequence, not as an independent argument for the triad. Its evidential weight lies in retrodiction. A standing puzzle that competing ontologies treat as brute is, on TRT, a structural expectation. A framework that turns an acknowledged anomaly into a prediction has thereby earned a measure of support, proportionate to how widely the anomaly is recognized and how poorly it is otherwise explained.

### 9.6 The Economy Claim, Stated Precisely

TRT posits three co-primitives. They are co-requiring constituents of actualized reality, each characterizable on its own terms, none obtaining in isolation. Their co-requirement is metaphysical, not definitional, so the theory is not circular. Their necessity is synthetic and conditional: given that something is actually real, it necessarily exhibits logical constraint, informational structure, and actualization, and the three come apart under analysis into three distinct failure modes, which is the mark of synthetic rather than analytic necessity. The claim rests on one stated posit, that actuality is a genuine status distinct from possibility, which TRT adopts openly and which leaves the physical content untouched under any admissible alternative. The effectiveness of mathematics follows as a consequence, since two of the three constituents are those from which mathematics arises. Nothing in this structure is circular, and nothing in it is removable without loss of the actual.

## 10. Implications for Physics

TRT suggests several research directions. First, physical law may be studied as an emergent stability structure within constrained informational action. Second, spacetime may be modeled as relational geometry derived from actualized informational order. Third, quantum measurement may be interpreted as logical resolution from admissible potentiality into determinate actuality. Fourth, energy may be reconceived as action-rate within emergent temporal order. Fifth, computational cosmology may need to distinguish computation from the deeper triadic structure that makes computation possible. Sixth, quantum gravity may benefit from treating logic, information, and action as co-foundational rather than treating spacetime as the initial stage.

The theory does not yet replace the formal machinery of quantum field theory, general relativity, or statistical mechanics. It provides a candidate ontological foundation beneath them.

### 10.1 Gravity as Relational Co-Admissibility

Gravity occupies a distinguished place within TRT. It is not merely one more actualized phenomenon among others. Every actualized phenomenon is logical, informational, and dynamic, since *χ* just is *A(I∞ | L₃)*. To say that gravity exhibits the triad therefore does not distinguish gravity from electromagnetism, the strong interaction, or any ordinary physical object. The relevant question is what is differentially logical, informational, and dynamic about gravity.

The differential fact is that gravity is geometric. The other interactions are ordinarily modeled as structures within spacetime. Gravity is the dynamical structure of spacetime itself. Since TRT has already characterized spacetime as the emergent relational geometry of actualized information (§6.3), the consequence is direct: gravity is not merely a content of *χ*, but the form of *χ*. It is the relational structure of actualized information.

This accounts, at the level of ontology, for gravity's universality and unscreenability. Everything actual participates in the relational structure of *χ*, so everything actual stands within the domain of gravity. There is no external standpoint from which an actualized system can screen itself from the relational geometry that constitutes its physical arena. Gravity differs from the content-forces because it is not merely an interaction among contents. It is the geometry in which contents are actualized.

The whole of this section is interpretive rather than derivational. Even the positioning claim, offered below at moderate confidence, explains why gravity has the features it has and why the information-geometric gravity programs succeed; it does not derive the field equations. The reader should not slide from this account to the stronger claim that TRT explains or derives gravity. The boundary between the two is the boundary the conjecture of §10.1.5 names and does not cross.

#### 10.1.1 The Informational Leg: Boundary and Area

The information associated with gravity is boundary information. The Bekenstein-Hawking result assigns black-hole entropy proportional to horizon area rather than enclosed volume. For a content-force, this would be surprising. For gravity, on the TRT reading, it is expected. If gravity is the relational geometry of actualized information, then the information relevant to a region's gravitational structure is encoded at the relational boundary between that region and its complement.

This connects TRT to the broader information-gravity research program, of which several independent instances exist. Jacobson (1995) derived the Einstein equation as a thermodynamic equation of state from horizon entropy proportional to area and the relation *δQ = T dS*. Ryu and Takayanagi (2006) proposed that entanglement entropy in a boundary conformal field theory is given holographically by the area of a minimal surface in the anti-de Sitter bulk. Bianconi (2025) proposes an action built from quantum relative entropy between the spacetime metric and a metric induced by matter fields. These programs differ in formal machinery, and TRT's reading does not depend on the success of any single one of them; they share a common intuition that gravitational structure is deeply tied to informational and boundary-defined quantities.

TRT's contribution at this level is interpretive rather than derivational. It says why informational and boundary-defined quantities should appear in gravity: gravity is the relational structure of actualized information, and relational structure is constituted at the boundary between a region and what it is related to.

#### 10.1.2 The Logical Leg: Gravity as Global Co-Admissibility

The logical constituent supplies the strongest differential claim. Gravity is the actualization of global co-admissibility in the relational structure of *χ*, whereas gauge theories impose consistency conditions on fields within an already structured relational arena.

This claim must be stated carefully. Global co-admissibility is defined independently of gravity. It is the requirement that the relations among actualized states jointly satisfy *L₃*. Gravity is then identified as the physical actualization of that independently specified requirement. The claim is not that gravity enforces gravitational consistency. The claim is that global logical consistency across relational structure is physically realized as gravitational structure.

General relativity gives this identification a concrete foothold. The twice-contracted second Bianchi identity states that the Einstein tensor is divergence-free:

$$\nabla_\mu G^{\mu\nu} = 0$$

This is a geometric identity. Once the field equation identifies *G_μν* with *8πG T_μν*, the covariant conservation of stress-energy follows:

$$\nabla_\mu T^{\mu\nu} = 0$$

The conservation condition is therefore not appended as an external law. It is built into the geometric constraint structure: the contracted Bianchi identity gives *∇_μ G^μν = 0*, and the field equation then entails *∇_μ T^μν = 0*. In the action formulation, this same structure is tied to diffeomorphism invariance through Noether identities.

This is a recognizable instance of the TRT claim. Global geometric self-consistency enforces a conservation condition on what is actualized. Conceptually, the requirement that the geometric side of the gravitational field equation be divergence-free is what makes the Einstein tensor the natural candidate. A consistency requirement on relational geometry constrains the form of gravitational structure.

The qualification is essential. Bianchi identities also appear in gauge theories. What distinguishes gravity is that the constrained geometric object is the dynamical relational arena itself. In gauge theories, consistency constrains fields within a background structure. In gravity, consistency constrains the relational structure that functions as the physical arena. This is why the claim remains an interpretive positioning claim rather than a completed derivation. Self-consistency yielding conservation is necessary to the picture, but it does not by itself single out the Einstein field equations from all possible theories possessing global constraints.

#### 10.1.3 The Dynamic Leg: Curvature as Relational Actualization Gradient

The content-forces produce transformations of states within the relational geometry. Gravity concerns the transformation of the geometry itself. On TRT, this means the restructuring of the relational order by actualizing action.

Energy was characterized in §6.2 as action-rate within emergent temporal order. The corresponding gravitational conjecture is that curvature encodes gradients in the density or rate of actualization across relational structure. Where actualization is uniform, the relational geometry is flat. Where actualization varies, the relational geometry curves.

This is schematic. It is not yet a derivation. The proposal requires a covariant measure of actualization density or actualization-rate, and no such measure has yet been supplied. Until that measure is defined, the identification of curvature with an actualization gradient remains a research direction, recorded among the open problems of §12.

#### 10.1.4 Black Holes as Exterior De-Actualization Boundaries

The informational, logical, and dynamic legs converge in black-hole physics. On TRT, a black-hole horizon may be interpreted, relative to the exterior relational domain, as the boundary at which outcome-actual availability terminates. Structure that crosses the horizon does not become inadmissible and does not pass into contradiction. Relative to exterior interaction, it ceases to be outcome-actual and becomes unresolved admissible structure for the exterior domain.

The arrow is therefore *χ_ext → I_{adm, ext}*, not *χ → I∞*. This restriction is load-bearing. The horizon does not mark a failure of *L₃*. It does not permit inadmissible structure. It marks a boundary of exterior outcome-accessibility. Determinacy relative to the exterior domain is lost, but admissibility is preserved.

This interpretation aligns with the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy at the level of ontology. The entropy measures hidden structure inaccessible to an exterior observer. On the TRT reading, that hidden structure is exterior-de-actualized structure: no longer outcome-actual to exterior interaction, but still admissible. That this entropy scales with horizon area rather than enclosed volume is expected if the loss of exterior-accessible determinacy occurs at the relational boundary.

The qualification is again essential. In standard general relativity, an infalling observer does not experience the horizon as a local de-actualization event. The horizon is not locally special; it is a causal boundary relative to exterior observers. TRT's black-hole claim is therefore not a claim that actuality vanishes at the horizon. It is a claim about outcome-actual accessibility relative to the exterior relational domain, and it must not be read as a claim about the local frame of an infalling system.

#### 10.1.5 Positioning Claim and Derivation Conjecture

The foregoing supports a positioning claim at moderate confidence and a derivation conjecture at low confidence.

*Positioning claim, confidence MEDIUM.* Gravity is the relational geometry of actualized information and, in its logical aspect, the actualization of global *L₃*-consistency across the relational structure of *χ*. This accounts for gravity's universality and unscreenability, for the boundary and area-scaling of gravitational entropy, and for the automatic, self-enforcing character of gravitational conservation through the contracted Bianchi identity. It also explains why entropic and entanglement-based gravity programs reach for information-geometric actions: if gravity is the relational structure of actualized information, then an action built on informational distinguishability is built from the correct class of primitive. The claim is interpretive; it grounds these features without deriving them.

*Derivation conjecture, confidence LOW, components UNCERTAIN.* The global co-admissibility of the relational structure of *χ*, once formalized, may force the constraint structure of general relativity rather than merely the broader genus of theories possessing global constraints. The target is the recovery of the divergence-free Einstein tensor and the field-equation form from a precise statement of global *L₃*-consistency on relational structure, or the connection of that statement to an information-geometric entropic action in an appropriate limit, including recovery of the black-hole area law with its coefficient. The conjecture fails if global consistency is compatible with non-Einstein constraint structures equally well, in which case the criterion underdetermines gravity and remains interpretive rather than derivational.

This is not yet a prediction. It becomes one only when global co-admissibility is formalized into a criterion that computes a definite gravitational structure.

## 11. Implications for Synthetic Intelligence

TRT also has implications for synthetic intelligence. If intelligence is understood merely as computation over data, then it is being modeled at a downstream level. Computation presupposes logical rules, informational states, and actual operations. These map naturally to the TRT triad.

Synthetic intelligence systems operate within *χ*. They process finite encoded information under formal constraints through energy-consuming action. They do not access *I∞* directly. They instantiate finite transformations within actualized reality.

This suggests a distinction between three levels: *I∞* (infinite informational potential); *I_encoded* (finite encoded information); and *I_processed* (information transformed by computational action). Generative AI belongs to the third category. It is a finite statistical transformation system operating within actualized reality, not consciousness, not pure reason, and not direct access to infinite information. TRT therefore gives a principled way to discuss AI without reducing mind, logic, or reality to computation.

## 12. Challenges and Open Problems

TRT faces several serious challenges.

The first challenge is formal precision. The primitives must be specified with sufficient rigor to avoid metaphor. *L₃*, *I∞*, *A*, and *χ* require formal definitions that can support mathematical development.

The second challenge is derivation. TRT must show how familiar physical structures can be derived or reconstructed from the triadic foundation. These include Hilbert space, probability, the Born rule, gauge symmetry, relativistic spacetime, field equations, and conservation laws. As noted in §5, the variational apparatus is at present a target rather than a result: the Lagrangian density *L* has no specified form, and no equations of motion have been derived from it.

A further challenge concerns the Born rule specifically. TRT locates the rule as the law governing the crossing from admissible structure to actualized outcome, but it does not derive the rule's form from the triadic structure. Whether the Born measure can be derived from the constraints on actualizing action, rather than assumed, is an open problem, and the measurement problem's hard residues, why one admissible branch actualizes rather than another on a given occasion, remain unsolved. TRT relocates these questions precisely; it does not dissolve them.

The third challenge is testability. A foundational ontology becomes scientifically meaningful only when it generates discriminating predictions, constrains model space, or clarifies existing anomalies in a way that competing frameworks do not. The co-admissibility conjecture of §13 is TRT's attempt to meet this challenge, and it is at present unproven.

The fourth challenge is dimensional analysis. The relation between ontological action *A*, physical action *S*, energy, and time must be developed carefully. The inference from the role of *S* in physics to the ontological role of *A*, flagged in §8.5, is part of this challenge.

The fifth challenge is avoiding equivocation. Logic, information, and action are each used in multiple disciplines with different meanings. TRT must define its terms before using them.

The sixth challenge is the relation to theology and metaphysics. TRT is metaphysically suggestive, and it carries one explicit metaphysical posit, stated in §9.3, that actuality is a genuine status distinct from possibility. The discipline required is to state such posits openly rather than to let them operate unannounced, and to keep clear which claims are formal, which are physical, and which are metaphysical. TRT can be compatible with theism, and perhaps strongly so, while observing that boundary. The standard it sets for itself is disclosure, not the absence of metaphysical commitment, since a foundational ontology without metaphysical commitment is not possible.

## 13. Falsifiability and Discriminating Conjectures

TRT, at the ontological level, is not falsified by a failed physical model derived from it. However, physics-level descendants of TRT can be tested. This section states the discriminating conjecture that the Logical Resolution Model generates, together with the broader research criteria by which the program should be judged.

### 13.1 The Co-Admissibility Conjecture

A foundational ontology earns the status of physics when it generates a claim that could be false and that distinguishes it from its rivals. TRT's first-stage account of measurement (§6.6.1), taken alone, does not meet this bar, since it agrees with decoherence on the cases decoherence settles. The discriminating claim must appear where decoherence is itself unsettled.

Such a place exists. When a system couples to two or more incompatible decoherence channels, non-commuting environmental couplings that select complementary observables, the preferred basis is not fixed by decoherence alone. The selection of pointer states in this regime is an open optimization problem, solved numerically against a chosen entanglement witness, and the answer depends in general on which witness is chosen. A representative case is a cavity field coupled simultaneously to a number-basis channel and a coherent-state-basis channel, where the einselected states interpolate between Fock and coherent states as the relative coupling varies, with Fock states remaining optimal up to a critical coupling obtainable analytically (Feller, Coeuret Cauquil and Roussel, 2020).

This regime is the natural test of the co-admissibility criterion, because here TRT's criterion and standard einselection are not guaranteed to agree, and the standard answer is measure-dependent where TRT's is meant to be measure-independent.

The conjecture is as follows.

> **Co-Admissibility Conjecture.** In the presence of incompatible decoherence channels, the set of co-admissible structures selected by the logical-compatibility criterion of LRM is determined by the constraint structure of the channels alone, without reference to a chosen entanglement witness, and coincides with the einselected set in the regimes where einselection is witness-independent while differing from it, in a specified and computable way, in the regimes where einselection is witness-dependent.

The conjecture is discriminating because it makes a definite claim about a case the standard theory leaves open, and because it can fail. It fails if the co-admissibility criterion, once formalized, either reproduces the witness-dependence it was meant to eliminate, or yields a co-admissible set that contradicts the experimentally accessible pointer structure in the cavity-field regime, including the analytically known critical coupling at which Fock-state optimality ends.

### 13.2 Status of the Conjecture

The conjecture is unproven, and it must be stated bluntly that it is not yet a prediction. It becomes a prediction only once co-admissibility is formalized into a criterion that computes a definite outcome. Until then it is a well-posed conjecture with a named target, not a result. Its confidence is LOW, and one component, whether the formalized criterion yields a unique and correct prediction rather than reproducing the standard optimization, is UNCERTAIN pending the formalization.

Two pieces of work stand between the conjecture and a result. The first is the formalization of co-admissibility into a criterion that computes a definite set of structures from a given collection of channel constraints, with no free measure. Until that formalization exists, the conjecture cannot be evaluated, and it is possible that the criterion, made precise, reduces to the einselection optimization and predicts nothing new. The second, contingent on the first, is the computation of the criterion's prediction for the cavity-field case with two incompatible channels and its comparison against the known critical coupling. A prediction that matches the analytic critical coupling would be a confirmation. A prediction that contradicts it would falsify the criterion. A criterion that cannot produce a definite prediction would show the proposal to be interpretation rather than physics.

TRT states the conjecture in this form, with its target named and its failure conditions specified, as the honest expression of a research program. It does not claim the prediction. It claims that the prediction is the thing to be sought, that the incompatible-channel regime is where to seek it, and that the analytically known features of that regime are what a successful criterion must reproduce.

### 13.3 Research Criteria

Beyond the specific conjecture, TRT should be judged by four research criteria. First, does it clarify the necessary conditions for physical actuality? Second, does it generate coherent mathematical descendants? Third, do those descendants recover known physics? Fourth, do they produce discriminating predictions? At present, TRT should be considered a foundational research program, not a completed physical theory.

### 13.4 The Gravity Co-Admissibility Conjecture

A second physics-facing conjecture concerns gravity, developed at length in §10.1. TRT's positioning claim identifies gravity as the relational geometry of actualized information and as the physical actualization of global co-admissibility across the relational structure of *χ*. The corresponding derivation conjecture is that a formal criterion of global co-admissibility will recover the constraint structure of general relativity, or connect to an information-geometric entropic action in an appropriate limit.

The conjecture has a definite target: derive or recover the divergence-free Einstein tensor, the field-equation form, and the black-hole area law, including its coefficient, from a formal statement of global *L₃*-consistency over relational structure.

The conjecture fails if the formalized criterion is compatible with multiple non-equivalent gravitational structures equally well, if it cannot distinguish Einstein gravity from the broader class of generally covariant theories, or if it cannot recover the known area law in the appropriate black-hole limit.

The confidence is LOW. The result is UNCERTAIN pending formalization. As with the co-admissibility conjecture of §13.1, the gravity conjecture is at present a named research target with stated failure conditions, not a derived prediction.

## 15. Conclusion

Triadic Reality Theory proposes that physical reality is neither brute material substance nor abstract law nor information alone. Physical reality is constrained information in action.

The finite laws of logic provide the conditions of coherent admissibility. Infinite informational potential provides the domain of possible distinction, relation, and structure. Actualizing action brings logically admissible possibility into determinate actuality. Their convergence is reality:

$$\chi \equiv \mathsf{A}(I_\infty \mid L_3)$$

The physical stability of that reality is expressed by *δS[χ] = 0*.

TRT offers a candidate foundational ontology beneath physics and metaphysics. Its strength lies in its economy: three co-requiring primitives, each a necessary constituent of actualized reality, none reducible to the others. Its weakness, at this stage, is its incompleteness as a formal physical theory. That weakness defines the research program. The task ahead is to move from ontological identity to mathematical derivation, and from mathematical derivation to discriminating prediction.

The thesis stands: reality is constrained information in action.
