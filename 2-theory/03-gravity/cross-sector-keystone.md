# The Cross-Sector Constraint on the Keystone

**Working document. What the quantum sector does — and does not — say about the gravity fork.**

**Status:** A structural argument, not a computation. **A first draft over-reached** (it claimed the quantum sector "leans" the gravity fork toward modified gravity); an independent red-team refuted that, and this version records the corrected, weaker result: a **conditional** cross-sector constraint that prices TRT's hoped-for unification, but **does not move the fork** and does **not** favor modified gravity. Tags: EARNED (rigorous), HEURISTIC (argued), ASSUMED (premise).

---

## 1. The keystone is shared (the program's own claim)

Both open conjectures turn on one unknown — how the actualizing primitive *A* resolves structure it cannot leave jointly *L₃*-satisfiable:

- **Gravity fork** ([`relational-graph-test.md`](relational-graph-test.md) §3): the tension of an unsatisfiable cycle is either the **raw holonomy deficit** `|δ|` (*linear* → Regge → exact GR) or the **best-fit least-squares residual** `δ²/3` (*quadratic* → Yang-Mills-type → modified gravity).
- **Quantum sector** ([`../02-variational/born-rule.md`](../02-variational/born-rule.md) §6.6): *A* resolves an unresolved superposition; the program factors this into **stage 1** (representable → co-admissible set; einselection, obstruction-flavored) and **stage 2** (co-admissible → single actual outcome; the Born selection, projective).

The keystone doc ([`../00-foundational/actualization-resolution.md`](../00-foundational/actualization-resolution.md)) states the program's hope: *the same A-nature governs both the Born measure and the gravity fork.* Note this is a shared **operator A**, asserted at MEDIUM confidence — **not** an established shared **value space**.

## 2. A structural switch sits behind both forks (HEURISTIC)

Both the gravity quadratic and the quantum projective structure presuppose **one** ingredient: **an inner product on the value space that *A* projects with.**

- The **linear / obstruction** measure `|δ|` is computed from the group operation alone — no inner product on *V*, no minimization.
- The **quadratic / projective** measure requires (i) a squared norm on *V* and (ii) a projection.

But the two quadratics are **not one operation** — that was the first draft's equivocation. They are *distinct projection functionals* of the same right triangle:

- gravity's `δ²/3` is the squared **residual**, `‖x − Px‖²` — the part of the constraint data that *could not be fit* (an obstruction-after-best-fit);
- Born's `|⟨ψ|φ⟩|²` is the squared **retained component**, `‖Px‖²` — the probability weight of an outcome the projection *kept*.

What they genuinely share is the *parent*: both are quadratic-because-projective, and both are inert without a metric on *V*. That weaker claim — **metric present ⇒ projective/quadratic structure available; metric absent ⇒ only raw obstruction** — is all the rest of the argument is entitled to use.

## 3. What the quantum sector actually establishes — and which stage it belongs to

The 2026-06-26 co-admissibility work pinned down that the *discriminating* quantum criterion uses the metric: the witness-free objects (commutant, kernel, generator spectrum) are non-discriminating, and the only measure-free object reproducing the einselected pointer structure is the purity-rate `γ̇`, the Hilbert–Schmidt sieve ([`../00-foundational/co-admissibility-formalization.md`](../00-foundational/co-admissibility-formalization.md), Attempts 2–4).

**Crucial stage attribution (the correction).** That metric-using object — `γ̇` — is the **stage-1** object (co-admissibility / einselection), and its session verdict was **re-description: it reproduces standard einselection and predicts nothing new** (Attempt 4). It is **not** the **stage-2** projective Born selection. And `born-rule.md` §6.6.2 explicitly treats the projective character of stage 2 as the **still-open keystone**, the "genuine remainder" TRT *does not derive*.

So the established fact is: *the **stage-1** value space (Hilbert) carries a metric, and the stage-1 criterion that re-describes einselection uses it.* This does **not**, by itself, establish that *A*'s **fundamental** nature is projective — that claim lives in stage 2, which TRT itself leaves open.

## 4. The constraint, stated correctly (a conditional, not a trilemma)

The first draft claimed an unconditional trilemma ("TRT cannot hold all three of {exact GR, projective measurement, shared-A unification}"). That is a **false trichotomy**, because a fourth combination holds all three at once — and it is TRT's own published position:

> **Keep all three:** *A* is **fundamentally obstruction-registering** (→ linear → exact GR), the projective Born statistics are a **stage-2 overlay** (the underived residue of `born-rule.md` §6.6.2), and *A* is shared across sectors. Cost: nothing beyond what the program already concedes (Born is not derived).

The genuine residue is a **conditional**:

> **If** (P1) the gravity and quantum sectors share a *value space* (not merely the operator *A*), **and** (P2) the projective Born structure is *fundamental to A* (not a stage-2 overlay), **then** exact GR, projective measurement, and the unification cannot all hold — and the metric the quantum sector demonstrably carries would force the gravity sector quadratic (modified gravity).

Both premises are unproven. **P2 is in tension with TRT's own `born-rule.md` §6.6.2**, which makes the projective layer the underived remainder. P1 is the stronger-than-asserted step (shared-V vs shared-A).

## 5. Which way it actually leans

**Undecided — and the leg under pressure is the unification, not exact GR.** The honest reading:

- The cross-sector view does **not** move the linear/quadratic fork. It re-weights an already-listed horn ("value space might be Hilbert → quadratic", `relational-graph-test.md` Gap C) *only* under the unproven shared-V premise.
- The empirical metric-use it points to is **stage-1 re-description** (verdict: predicts nothing new), not the stage-2 projective nature the modified-gravity conclusion would need. Using a result whose own headline was "predicts nothing new" to predict something new in another sector is the over-reach the red-team flagged (the same pattern caught in Attempt 3).
- The cheapest reconciliation (the §4 fourth combination) keeps **both** exact GR **and** the unification, at the already-accepted cost of an underived Born rule. So if anything the GR horn is *not* weakened.

The real, modest contribution: the cross-sector view **prices TRT's unification.** To buy the strong unification (shared value space + a fundamentally projective *A*) is to give up exact GR. To keep exact GR, TRT either treats projective Born as a stage-2 overlay (its current position — keeping GR and a *weak* shared-*A* unification, but leaving Born underived) or denies shared-V (keeping GR, losing the unification). That trade was implicit before; it is now explicit.

## 6. Honest gaps / what would change the verdict

- **G1 — same value space (P1).** Demonstrating the gravity relational *V* carries/inherits the Hilbert inner product (not just the operator *A*) would activate the strong horn. Unpaid.
- **G2 — Born fundamental to *A* (P2).** Showing *from within TRT* that stage-2 projective Born is *A*'s intrinsic operation, not the conceded overlay, would collapse the GR escape and make the modified-gravity lean live. This directly contradicts a current `born-rule.md` claim, so it would itself be a major result. Unpaid; the highest-leverage move.
- **G3 — one variational parent.** If the gravity residual `‖x−Px‖²` and the Born coefficient `‖Px‖²` were shown to descend from a *single* variational functional (not merely "both inner-product-quadratic"), §2's kinship would strengthen toward identity. As it stands they are distinct functionals.

## 7. Status

A HEURISTIC structural argument, corrected after red-team. It does **not** decide the gravity fork and does **not** move it toward modified gravity. Its contribution is to make explicit a **conditional cross-sector constraint**: the strong unification (shared value space + fundamentally-projective *A*) is incompatible with exact GR, so TRT's hoped-for "two problems, one answer" is *priced* — the leg at risk is the unification (or shared-V), not exact GR. **OPN-002 / PRD-002 stay open; no confidence move.** Next leverage is G2 (is Born fundamental to *A* or overlay?), which is the same stage-2 keystone the whole program already turns on.
