## 6.6 The Logical Resolution Model

The reconciled §6.5 located measurement as the crossing from admissible structure to actualized outcome and named the Logical Resolution Model as the account of that crossing. This file specifies what LRM proposes. The proposal has two parts, and they carry very different evidential weight.

The crossing decomposes into two stages. The first runs from the representable to the admissible, *I∞ → I_adm*. The second runs from the admissible to the actual, *I_adm → χ*. LRM makes a substantive claim about the first stage and an honest concession about the second.

### 6.6.1 The First Stage: Resolution to the Admissible Set

When a quantum system couples to physical state variables — an apparatus, an environment, a set of conserved quantities — the interaction does not leave every mathematically representable structure standing. The coupling imposes constraints, and most of the infinite representable structure cannot satisfy them. What survives is the set of structures that remain logically co-admissible under the full constraint imposed by the physical variables in interaction.

This survival criterion is the content of the phrase that measurement is that which survives logical interaction with the state variables. The survivors are not selected by an observer. They are selected by the logical compatibility of candidate structures with the totality of constraints the actual physical variables impose. The criterion is co-admissibility rather than mere consistency: the survivors form the maximal set of structures that can *jointly* satisfy *L₃* given the variables, not merely the structures individually consistent with any one constraint.

This first-stage claim recovers, in TRT's terms, a result that decoherence theory establishes independently. Environmental monitoring suppresses coherence between a preferred set of states and bans the rest of Hilbert space from stable classical existence. The pointer states that survive einselection are the physically robust survivors of interaction with the environment. TRT reads these as the co-admissible set: the structures that survive logical interaction with the physical variables. The agreement with decoherence is the reason the first-stage claim is offered with **moderate confidence**. It is not a new prediction. It is a re-description, in logical-ontological terms, of selection that decoherence already explains dynamically.

The value of the re-description, if it has value, is twofold. It grounds the selection in *L₃* rather than in a choice of entanglement measure, and it removes the circularity that has been charged against the einselection program, since co-admissibility is defined by logical compatibility with the variables rather than by stability under a dynamics whose preferred basis the argument is trying to explain. Whether this grounding is more than re-description is exactly the question the [co-admissibility conjecture](../../3-prediction/co-admissibility-conjecture.md) is designed to decide.

### 6.6.2 The Second Stage: Actualization of the Outcome

The first stage resolves the representable to the co-admissible set. In general this set has more than one member. The number-basis pointer states of a decohered field are a co-admissible set, not a single state. Something further selects the one outcome that actualizes on a given occasion.

LRM does not explain that final selection. The transition from the co-admissible set to the single actual outcome, *I_adm → χ*, is the residual actualization event, the operation of *A* on an occasion. TRT locates it, names it, and assigns it the Born rule as its probabilistic law, but it does not derive the Born measure and does not say what fixes the particular outcome beyond its probability. This is the hard residue of the measurement problem. The factoring is the contribution: the first stage is logical and, TRT claims, explicable; the second stage is the genuine remainder, and TRT does not pretend to have removed it.

**This second stage is the keystone question** — see [`../00-foundational/actualization-resolution.md`](../00-foundational/actualization-resolution.md). Whether *A* resolves by obstruction-registration (linear) or best-fit projection (quadratic) governs both the Born measure here and the gravity fork in [`../03-gravity/`](../03-gravity/).
