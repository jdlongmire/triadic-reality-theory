# The Actualization-Resolution Question (Keystone)

> **Tier:** 2-theory (protective belt), but the **shared root** of Task 2.2 (Born rule), Task 3.1 (gravity fork), and the LRM. **Lakatos role:** the single unknown on which two of the program's open conjectures both turn. **Confidence that this is the shared keystone:** MEDIUM — the honest product of the relational-graph computation; the structural parallel between the gravity fork and measurement resolution emerged from the computation rather than being assumed.

## Why this is the keystone

The relational-graph computation ([`../03-gravity/relational-graph-test.md`](../03-gravity/relational-graph-test.md)) reduced the gravity fork to a single question about the primitive *A*: when a relational structure is not jointly *L₃*-satisfiable, how does actualizing action resolve it?

- If *A* registers the obstruction directly (the raw holonomy deficit), the measure is **linear**, and the gravity sector yields General Relativity (via Regge / Lovelock).
- If *A* resolves by best-fit projection (minimizing a squared residual), the measure is **quadratic**, and the gravity sector yields a modified, higher-curvature gravity (Bianconi-class).

The same operation appears in the quantum sector: the Logical Resolution Model ([`../02-variational/born-rule.md`](../02-variational/born-rule.md)) describes how *A* resolves an unresolved superposition into a determinate outcome. Measurement is often modeled as a projection. So the gravity fork and the measurement-resolution problem may be governed by the same unknown.

## The question, stated precisely

Characterize *A*'s resolution operation on unsatisfiable / unresolved admissible structure. Specifically: is it (a) **obstruction-registering** (linear, metric-free, uses only the relational/group structure), or (b) **best-fit projective** (quadratic, requires an inner product on the value space and a minimization)?

## What each answer entails

| *A*'s nature | Gravity sector | Quantum sector | Measure |
|---|---|---|---|
| Obstruction-registering | General Relativity | (to be worked out) | linear |
| Best-fit projective | Modified gravity | projective measurement | quadratic |

## Sub-routes (from relational-graph-test.md §9)

1. **Formal:** define *A*'s resolution operation; if projective, determine the norm and whether it is forced or imposed. Connects to Task 2.2 (Born rule).
2. **Comparative-observational:** linear and quadratic differ at high curvature; the fork becomes the empirical question of whether higher-curvature corrections exist. See [`../../3-prediction/gravity-linearity-test.md`](../../3-prediction/gravity-linearity-test.md).

## Failure condition (`risk_if_false`)

If *A*'s resolution operation cannot be characterized, or is shown to be best-fit projective with an imposed norm, the linear/GR route closes and TRT's gravity is modified gravity. Record plainly.
